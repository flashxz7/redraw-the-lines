Key Legal Cases
Rucho v. Common Cause
588 U.S. 684 (2019)
The Supreme Court ruled 5–4 that federal courts cannot adjudicate claims of partisan gerrymandering — such claims present "political questions" beyond federal judicial reach. State courts and independent redistricting commissions remain the primary legal remedy.
Gill v. Whitford
585 U.S. ___ (2018)
Challenge to Wisconsin's 2011 REDMAP gerrymander (EG ≈ 11.7%). The Supreme Court unanimously remanded on standing grounds, never reaching the merits. Wisconsin later redrew its maps under state court order.
Wesberry v. Sanders
376 U.S. 1 (1964)
Established the "one person, one vote" requirement for congressional districts under Article I. Congressional districts must be as equal in population as practicable — no more than ~0.1% deviation allowed.
Abbott v. Perez
585 U.S. 579 (2018)
Supreme Court largely upheld Texas's 2011 congressional maps but struck down one district as a racial gerrymander under the Voting Rights Act, illustrating how racial and partisan gerrymandering analysis often overlap.
Key Academic Sources
Stephanopoulos & McGhee (2015) "Partisan Gerrymandering and the Efficiency Gap." 82 U. Chi. L. Rev. 831. Introduced the efficiency gap metric; proposed 7% as the presumptive threshold for problematic maps.
Wisconsin Legislative Reference Bureau Wisconsin 2011 REDMAP redistricting history and court record. EG was approximately 11.7% — cited in Gill v. Whitford briefs.
REDMAP Report (RSLC, 2012) Republican State Leadership Committee's own post-election report documenting the coordinated strategy to control state legislatures ahead of the 2010 redistricting cycle.
A Note on This Tool
This tool uses county-level geographic data (254 TX counties) as drawing units. Real redistricting uses census blocks or precincts (~9,000 in TX) for far finer control. County-level drawing overstates how "blocky" maps must look and understates how precisely district lines can be drawn to achieve partisan goals. Vote data is derived from Plan C2193 district-level 2020 presidential returns distributed proportionally across counties — actual precinct data would be more accurate. This is an educational approximation.
